  To solve the equation \(5x + 2 = 3\) for \(x\), follow these steps:
  
  1. **Isolate the term containing \(x\):**  
     Subtract 2 from both sides of the equation to move the constant term to the right side.  
     \[
     5x + 2 - 2 = 3 - 2
     \]  
     Simplify:  
     \[
     5x = 1
     \]
  
  2. **Solve for \(x\):**  
     Divide both sides by 5 to isolate \(x\).  
     \[
     \frac{5x}{5} = \frac{1}{5}
     \]  
     Simplify:  
     \[
     x = \frac{1}{5}
     \]
  
  **Verification:**  
  Substitute \(x = \frac{1}{5}\) back into the original equation to check:  
  \[
  5 \left( \frac{1}{5} \right) + 2 = 1 + 2 = 3
  \]  
  The result is 3, which matches the right side of the equation. Thus, the solution is correct.
  
  The solution is \(x = \frac{1}{5}\). This can also be expressed as \(x = 0.2\) in decimal form, but the fraction is exact and preferred unless specified otherwise.
