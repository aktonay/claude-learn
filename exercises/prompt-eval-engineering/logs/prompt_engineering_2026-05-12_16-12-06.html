Key Takeaways

Step 1: Be Clear and Direct
A naive prompt with no guidance. The model has to guess what format, detail level, and structure you want. Expect low, inconsistent scores.
Step 2: Be Specific
Adding explicit guidelines (calorie count, macro breakdown, meal timing, portion sizes) gives the model a clear target. This typically produces the largest score jump.
Step 3: Use XML Tags
Wrapping content in XML tags like <athlete_information> creates clear boundaries. The model can distinguish instructions from data, reducing confusion. Impact grows with prompt complexity.
Step 4: Provide Examples
A sample input/output pair shows the model exactly what "good" looks like. This is especially powerful for format, tone, and detail-level calibration. Find your best-scored outputs from evaluations and use them as examples.

The evaluation pipeline makes this process objective. Instead of "this prompt feels better," you get: Step 1 scored X, Step 2 scored Y, Step 3 scored Z, Step 4 scored W. Each technique's contribution is measurable.
