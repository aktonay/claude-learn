1.  Let the cost of the ball be **B**.
2.  The bat costs $1.00 more than the ball, so the cost of the bat is **B + $1.00**.
3.  Together, they cost $1.10, so we can write the equation:
    B + (B + $1.00) = $1.10

*   Combine the terms with B:
    2B + $1.00 = $1.10
*   Subtract $1.00 from both sides:

*   Divide both sides by 2:
    B = $0.05

So, the ball costs **$0.05** (5 cents), and the bat costs **$1.05**. This adds up to $1.10, and the bat is exactly $1.00 more expensive than the ball.
